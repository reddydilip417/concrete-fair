ConCube

Concrete is ubiquitous. One cannot imagine a life without concrete. A Civil Engineer starts his career by learning to design a concrete mix. This traditional event predominantly focuses on the adroitness of the participant right from designing the mix until casting the cube.

Rules:

  • Teams of two; cross-college teams not allowed.
  • Written preliminary round followed by the final cube-casting round.

  • Decision of the judges shall be final and binding.

Conpress

The Paper presentation symposium is an opportunity for students to present their research ideas based solely on concrete before some of the nation’s most celebrated and eminent professors, students and industry professionals. This national level event provides a platform to share and present innovations in concrete.

Rules:
  • The event is open to both UG and PG Civil Engineering students.
  • Maximum of 2 students can make a presentation on a single topic. In such a case, both members must have made a significant contribution to the research work.
  • The theme of the competition is: 'Advancements in Concrete Technology'. Participants can present research paper on any topic confirming to the above theme.
  • Abstract of the research paper should be sent in pdf format to concretefair2015@gmail.com by 5.00pm October 6, 2015.
  • The Abstract should contain the Title, Full Name of author(s) (separated by comma in case of multiple authors), text of the Abstract (word count between150 and 300) and Keywords.
  • Please note that this is a screening stage; Abstracts received will be reviewed by our review panel before selecting for presentation at ConPress conclave. Therefore, make sure to send high quality version of your Abstract.
  • Selected candidates must send their full paper in pdf format to concretefair2015@gmail.com by 5.00pm October 12, 2015. Papers should confirm to the ASCE format. The final report/paper must not exceed 10 pages. It is advised to prepare for final presentation without waiting for the Abstract Screening Stage results, in order not to lose out on time.
  • Each selected candidate/team will have total time of 10 minutes to make a PowerPoint presentation during ConPress (7 minutes for presentation; 3 minutes for Q and A)
  • Selected candidates must bring both a hard copy and a soft copy of the final report, along with their PPT on the day of the event.

Here’s your opportunity to show off your skills!!

CADD U QUANTIFY?

Civil Engineers are the ones who can estimate, calculate and design things by just looking at it. On this basis, here we aim at testing the skills of the students in designing and drafting using Auto CADD along with the basic concepts in estimation. Entry permitted for UG students only.


Time Duration
  • 1st Round: 30 Mins.
  • 2nd Round: 1 Hour.
Max participation: Team of 2
Event Rules and reguations: Round 1:
Each team will be provided with a building plan along with dimensions. Based on that plan 10-15 questions will be framed which are related to basic estimation and which are practical oriented. Participants should have to answer those questions within a limited time of 30 minutes. There will be a * marked questions which are considered in case of tie between 2 teams. Details about the materials will be provided.
Top 15 teams from this round will be qualified for round-2
Rules:
  • Each team would have to complete the challenge in the specified time duration of 30 minutes.
  • Teams will not be allowed to access the intern or any reference books or materials during this round.
  • Use of mobile phones is strictly prohibited.
  • Each team is allowed to access calculator (Simple or scientific) during this round.
  • The score obtained by each team will be disclosed.
Round 2:
The top 15 teams from Round-1 will be eligible for this round.
Each team will be given with a line diagram of a building plan. They are required to draw the plan and cross action including wall thickness of the given building line diagram on the Auto CADD. In the speculated time of 60 minutes they are also required to estimate the number of Bricks, Tiles, and Concrete etc. of any given area in the plan.
Details such as size of the Brick, Tiles, Dimensions of the room, hall, etc will be given. Each team must submit the plan cross section and the estimate within 60 minutes.
Rules:
  • Each team would have to complete the challenge in the specified time duration of 60 minutes.
  • Teams will not be allowed to access the intern or any reference books or materials during this round.
  • Each team is allowed to access calculator (Simple or scientific) during this round.
  • Each team is required to depict the size of the room, hall etc. on the plan.
  • The score obtained by each team may or may not be disclosed.

Step the zone

This flagship event of Concrete Fair, 2016 takes the participants on a ride through the core domains of building construction. Technical skills and a bit of wit is all the participants need to ace this competition. All this fun learning is packed into multiple rounds, each of which is based on a Civil Engineering concept. At the end of each round, the teams with the lowest scores will be eliminated. The last team standing takes home the big cash prize and a host of exciting goodies.


Time Duration
  • Zone 1: 30 Mins.
  • Zone 2: 1.5 hours.
  • Zone 3: 1.5 hours
  • Zone 4: 2 hours
Max participation: Team of 3
Event Rules and reguations:
Zone 1:
All the teams will be given a map with three different regions. These regions have varying soil strength. Here the scenario is that there will be a pile foundation in each of these regions. The reaction due to skin friction in each soil will be given to the teams. They have to calculate the depth of the piles required at each location given a fixed diameter. Each foundation has four piles. The load of the super-structure at each foundation will be different.
Rules:
  • The question will be given to the teams along with a working sheet.
  • Use of scientific calculators is permitted.
  • The first 20 teams that provide the correct answer will qualify for zone 2.
Zone 2:
The teams are required to build a structure using straws and cello tape. Each team has 50 straws to construct a structure that is at least 20 cm tall on a cardboard square. The teams with top 6 efficiencies will go to the next zone. Efficiency is the ratio of failure load to the self-weight. The load will be applied at the top of the tower using a container. The load will be increased by pouring in sand at a constant rate.
Rules:
  • The team leader will have to collect the materials for the event.
  • Any query or requirement that the team has should be communicated to the organisers by the team leader only.
  • There is provision for extra cello tape but not for straws.
  • The participating teams are not allowed to use the equipment or materials provided to other teams.
  • Only one cardboard base will be provided. (No extras in case the teams damage it.)
  • The testing will be done in the presence of the team. It is the team’s responsibility to transport the model to the testing area.
  • The efficiency will be calculated by the organisers after the self-weight and failure load are informed to the teams.
  • The failure load will be the load at which the container at the top falls to the ground.
  • The load will be applied by the organisers under the observation the teams
  • The top six teams which achieve the highest efficiency will qualify for zone 3.
Zone 3:
The teams will be required to measure the distance and the difference in elevation between two points but the trick is that there will be a large obstruction between the points. They will be provided with a dumpy level, levelling staff, tape, cross staff and ranging rods.
Rules:
  • The team leader will have to collect the materials and equipment for the event.
  • It’s the teams’ responsibility to check the condition of the equipment before starting the survey.
  • Any query or requirement that the team has should be communicated to the organisers by the team leader only.
  • The participating teams are not allowed to use the equipment or materials provided to other teams.
  • Any team that damages the equipment or college property will incur fines or might even be disqualified from the event.
  • Judging will be done based on the accuracy of their result. In case of a tie, the time taken to complete the task will be considered.
  • The top 3 teams advance to the final zone.
Zone 4:
The participants will be given a case which they have to analyse with their engineering skills. After the analysing the case they have to propose a solution to the problem by using the journal papers that we give them. A large number of research papers which may or may not be related to the case are given to them. They have to read the ones that are most relevant to their case to present a well justified solution.
Rules:
  • The case will be given to the teams on a sheet of paper.
  • A computer/laptop containing the research papers will be provided to each team.
  • Any technical difficulties with the computers should be brought to the attention of the organisers immediately by the team leader. Additional time equal to the time lost in resolving the technical issue will provided.
  • The teams must not access any other files on the computers.
  • They must refrain from internet usage on the computers.
  • Each team will have 75 mins to study the case and come up with a solution. After 75 mins, the teams will have to leave the computers and move to the waiting area.
  • The teams will be called at random to give verbal presentation. Laptops will be provided if the teams need to show graphs, tables etc.
  • The time limit for presentation is 10 mins.
  • The participants will be judged on the analysis of the case (50pts), understanding the problem points (50pts) and providing a well justified solution (100pts).
  • The decision of the judges will be final and binding.
